Metaphysics

Concise guide to the study of what there is, how it is, and why it matters.

Quick start

  1. Read what metaphysics studies and why it is distinct from physics or theology.
  2. Scan the core questions to map the territory.
  3. Pick one subfield and work through one classic text plus one modern summary.

Time: 90–120 minutes for a first pass.

What is metaphysics

Metaphysics is the branch of philosophy that studies the most general features of reality: what exists, what it is like, and how different kinds of things relate. It asks questions that sit “behind” the sciences and everyday claims, clarifying the categories we use to describe the world.

  • Ontology: inventory of what exists and the structure of being.
  • Modality: possibility, necessity, and laws.
  • Identity and change: what makes something the same over time.
  • Causation and explanation: dependence, powers, and why things happen.
  • Space and time: their reality and relation to objects and events.
  • Mind and matter: how consciousness fits into the world.

Core questions

  • What kinds of things are fundamental?
  • Are numbers, properties, or universals real?
  • What is a law of nature?
  • Could the world have been otherwise?
  • What is personal identity across time?
  • Do causes produce effects or merely correlate?
  • Is time tensed (past/present/future) or tenseless?
  • How does mind relate to brain?

Subfields

Ontology

Categories of being, mereology (parts/wholes), realism vs. nominalism.

Modality

Possible worlds, essence, necessity, counterfactuals.

Identity & change

Persistence, four-dimensionalism vs. endurantism, ship of Theseus.

Causation

Regularity, counterfactual, and powers accounts; causal graphs.

Space & time

Substantivalism vs. relationism; presentism vs. eternalism.

Mind & world

Dualism, physicalism, panpsychism; grounding of consciousness.

Methods

  • Conceptual analysis: clarify terms and infer commitments.
  • Thought experiments: test intuitions under controlled variation.
  • Formal tools: modal logic, set theory, graph models of dependence.
  • Inference to best explanation: compare theory cost vs. explanatory power.
  • Cross-checks: coherence with science and with ordinary language.

Relations to science

Metaphysics is not empirical science but interacts with it by interpreting scientific results and proposing frameworks within which results make sense. Physics informs debates on time, cosmology, and laws; cognitive science informs debates on mind.

Critiques & limits

  • Verificationism: claims without testable consequences are meaningless.
  • Quinean critique: ontology should follow our best science.
  • Method worries: heavy reliance on intuitions can mislead.

Good practice: state assumptions, mark uncertainty, and show dependency graph of claims.

Study roadmap

  1. Primer: SEP or IEP article on Metaphysics.
  2. Classic: Aristotle Metaphysics (selections) or Descartes Meditations.
  3. Modern: van Inwagen Metaphysics, Lowe A Survey of Metaphysics.
  4. Method: Williamson The Philosophy of Philosophy chs. on methodology.
  5. Project: pick one subfield and build a one-page thesis card with sources.

FAQs

Is metaphysics obsolete after modern physics?

No. Physics answers empirical questions; metaphysics clarifies categories and assumptions in which those answers live.

Isn’t it just speculation?

Bad metaphysics is. Good work states assumptions, argues from costs/benefits, and respects science.
