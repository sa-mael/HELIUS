About us

HELIUS is a living handbook of changing truth. We help people form positions fast with clear arguments, risks, and verifiable sources.

What we do

  • Capture theses as cards with tabs: Summary · Versions · Sources · Discuss.
  • Link theses into an ontology: support, refutation, dependency.
  • Show dynamics over time with a time-scrubber across versions.
  • Provide thinking tools: Bias toggles, What-if scenarios, voting.
  • Score impact with confidence labels and author reputation.
  • Export to PDF/PNG with correct citations; offer API and embeds.

Who it's for

  • Individuals: students, creators, content analysts.
  • Teams: newsrooms, research groups, nonprofits, product teams.
  • Education & think tanks: critical-thinking courses, seminars, debates.

How it works

  1. Select topics and subscribe to streams.
  2. Open a thesis card to see summary, versions, sources, and discussion.
  3. Toggle biases, scrub time, and run “what-if” checks.
  4. Vote, save, and share a cited thesis card.

Our story

  • Origin: a debate club drowning in links. We split arguments into thesis cards and it worked.
  • MVP: 20 theses, public diffs, voting, export. First users were lecturers and editors.
  • Growth: ontology links, confidence labels, private team spaces, API and embed widget.
  • Now: building a versioned thesis base with open change history and author reputation.

Impact

  • Less noise: claims ship with sources.
  • Faster decisions: positions form through versions and counter-arguments.
  • Transparency: changes are visible in diffs and over time.
  • Skill-building: users practice evidence comparison and risk framing.
  • Team effect: reviews and a shared format reduce unproductive debate.

Principles

  • Sources before opinions.
  • Versions over finality.
  • Public diffs and confidence labels.
  • Zero tolerance for personal attacks.
  • User data is not a product; clear privacy controls.

Source policy & moderation

  • Documented acceptance criteria for sources.
  • Mandatory citations with caps on verbatim quotes.
  • Confidence labels at thesis and version levels.
  • Appeals and an edits journal.

Author reputation

  • Reputation grows via verifiable theses, precise updates, and quality sources.
  • Citations and reviews weigh more than likes.
  • Author reputation is distinct from topic reputation.

Quality metrics

  • Meaningful reads: >45s on card and ≥1 source opened.
  • Activation: first-day vote plus ≥3 sources opened.
  • Retention: returns to topics and versions per thesis.
  • UGC share, review rate, ontology link depth.

Safety & legal

  • Content licenses, DMCA process, explicit disclaimers.
  • Separate moderation ledger; audit trails for orgs.
  • Private spaces, SSO, and change audits for teams.

How to work with us

  • Individuals: read, save, subscribe to topics.
  • Creators: publish theses, maintain versions, connect sources.
  • Teams: review in private spaces, export briefs.
  • Partners & EDU: embed the widget in blogs and courses; run workshops.

Our commitment

  • Transparent sources.
  • Honest corrections with dates.
  • Explainable ranking and reputation logic.

Topics and streams

Subscribe to streams you care about. Each card shows scope, methods, and core sources.

Economics

Markets, policy, trade, labor, incentives.

  • Questions: inflation vs. unemployment, productivity, wages.
  • Methods: time-series, natural experiments, cost–benefit.
  • Indicators: CPI, PPI, GDP, unemployment, real wages.

Core sources: IMF, World Bank, OECD, BLS, BEA, FRED.

Technology

AI, cybersecurity, platforms, open source, regulation.

  • Risks: alignment, privacy, misuse, critical infrastructure.
  • Benchmarks: reliability, safety evals, data lineage.
  • Trade-offs: speed vs. governance, openness vs. abuse.

Core sources: arXiv, NIST, ISO/IEC, CSET.

Geopolitics

Security, alliances, sanctions, energy corridors.

  • Frames: power balance, deterrence, regime type.
  • Levers: trade controls, aid, diplomacy, information.
  • Watchlists: Europe, Indo-Pacific, Middle East.

Core sources: UN, NATO, trade stats, think tanks.

Health & Science

Evidence hierarchies, trials, population health.

  • Studies: RCTs, cohort, case-control, meta-analysis.
  • Quality: pre-registration, blinding, effect sizes, bias.
  • Outcomes: mortality, QALYs, adverse events.

Core sources: WHO, NIH, Cochrane, PubMed.

Climate & Energy

Emissions, grids, storage, transition policy.

  • Scopes: 1 (direct), 2 (electricity), 3 (value chain).
  • Grid needs: firm power, transmission, demand response.
  • Metrics: LCOE, capacity factor, lifecycle intensity.

Core sources: IPCC, IEA, EIA, national inventories.

Law & Policy

Rights, courts, governance, implementation.

  • Levels: constitutional, statutory, regulatory, case law.
  • Cycle: agenda → drafting → comment → enforcement → review.
  • Tests: strict scrutiny, proportionality, administrative record.

Core sources: registers, court opinions, OECD reviews.
